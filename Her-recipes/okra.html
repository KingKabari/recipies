How to make sea food okra

From the Igbo language of southeastern Nigeria we get the vegetable’s English name, as okwuru became ochra and okra. Along the entire length of the 3,500 mile coast exploited by the trans-Atlantic slave trade, okra was grown and cooked with other vegetables or rice and made into soup.

prep time:45 mins
cook time:25 mins
servings:4

Ingredients

  • 25 pieces medium sized Fresh Okro
  • 2 Stock cubes
  • 1 cup Palm oil
  • 1 tablespoon Ground pepper
  • 3-4 Yellow Scotch bonnet
  • ¼ cup Ground Crayfish
  • 200g Prawns deveined , cleaned
  • 1 whole Mackerel or Hake fish cut, cleaned
  • 4 Crabs
  • 1 cup Vegetable (Ugu or spinach)
  • Salt to taste.

Instructions

  1. Wash the Okro and grate or blend into small cubes. If blending, be careful not to use too much water.
  2. In a pot, add about 2 cups of water, add in the crabs, stock cubes, pepper, palmoil, and salt
  3. Leave to cook for 10 minutes then add crayfish, fish, shrimps and leave to cook for another 10 minutes.
  4. Take out the fish from the sauce(Don’t worry this will be introduced back into the pot. Reason is not to scatter the tender fish.)
  5. Now add the grated or blended okro and Ugu leaves.
  6. Taste for salt and seasoning, adjust accordingly, reduce the heat and let simmer for 5 minutes.
  7. Add the fish taken out in step 4 back to the pot
  8. Serve and enjoy with swallow of choice.
