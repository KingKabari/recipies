How to make Nigerian meat pie

one of Nigeria’s popular snack and it’s loved by all. The buttery shortcrust pastry is filled with mincemeat cooked in rich flavour. It is delicious and you won't be able to stop at one. Do you want to learn how to make a perfect meat pie? this recipe is for you

prep time:30 mins
bake time:30 mins
servings:10

Ingredients

  • 500 g Mince meat or ground beef (18-20%fat)
  • 700 g Plain flour, extra for dusting and thickening700 g Plain flour, extra for dusting and thickening
  • 380 g unsalted butter
  • 1 large egg for egg wash
  • water 4 to 8 tbsp (100 ml)
  • 1 beef bullion cube (I used knorr stock cube use any of choice)
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 small onion chopped (about ⅓ cup)
  • 2 medium potatoes peeled and cubed (about 1 cup)
  • 1 big carrot cleaned and cubed (about ½ cup)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp sugar optional
  • salt to taste (use ½ tsp for the pastry)

Instructions

    To make the dough

  1. Rub together till all turn to crumbs
  2. Add 100 ml of water and mix till all is combined. If the dough still needs water add a tablespoon of water at a time till you get well combined dough
  3. Set dough aside in a cool place or a fridge for 30 minutes then you move on to preparing the meaty filling.
  4. Yes! Making the dough is that easy but remember, DO NOT overwork it when handling.

    To make the pie filling

  1. Place a pan on medium heat and add oil, heat for 2 minutes, add chopped onions and fry till onion is translucent. Add mince to the onion and stir mince till its brown then add curry powder, thyme, stock cube, a pinch of salt and stir till all combine. Reduce the heat if need be to avoid burning.
  2. The fat from the mince should make the sauce juicy but if it looks dry, add 4 water to it. (decant oil if it is too much)
  3. Add boiled potatoes and carrots, combine well and cook on a low heat for another 3-5 minutes.
  4. The mince should be well cooked now. Now you move on to thickening the mince.
  5. Add 2tbsp of flour to a bowl and mix with water to form a paste. Add the paste to the mince meat on the heat and combine well. Leave to cook for another minute. Take off the heat and allow to cool.

    Rolling the dough and making the meat pie

  1. Preheat oven at 180C/350F
  2. Crack the egg into a bowl, whisk and set aside
  3. Sprinkle a bit of flour on a plain, clean surface/worktop
  4. Divide dough into 2 or 3 places and roll out flat using a rolling pin(should be 0.5 -1 cm thick)
  5. Place a circle cutter on the flat dough and cut out into many circles you can get. use a makeshift if you don’t have a cutter. Repeat this process with the rest of the dough
  6. Add the meat filling to the centre of the circled dough, brush one half of the circle with whisked egg and carefully fold over the meat filling making sure the 2 edges touch one another. The aim of the egg is to seal the dough.
  7. Close edges by pressing down with the tip of a fork
  8. Repeat this process for the remaining dough and filling.
  9. set on a baking tray, egg wash the surface of the pasteries and poke through with a fork
  10. Place in the oven and bake for 20-30 minutes at 180C⁰/350F.
Note:
  • You can also use salted butter for this recipe. If you would be using it, please omit salt from making the shortcrust pastry.
  • You can add a bit of water to the mince filling if it looks too thick or dry.
  • Allow the meat pie filling to cool down completely before adding to pie dough.
