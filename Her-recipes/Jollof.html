How to make Jollof rice

A dish that requires simple steps, but is seemingly complicated. To some people, it comes naturally whilst for some others it’s a journey to master just the basics. However, I say it’s a journey that is worth it all! A delicious dish that encompasses: Blitz, fry, simmer, and steam, no more, no less. This recipe will guide even the novice of novices to embark on this delicious journey.

prep time:25 mins
cook time:65 mins
servings 4
calories: 470 kcal

Ingredients

  • 8 tomatoes
  • 5 scotch bonnet pepper
  • 2 red onion
  • 2 thumb-size fresh ginger
  • 1 sachet tomato paste
  • 0.5 cup oil (any kind would do)
  • 2 cup long-grain rice (Washed)
  • 3 tbsp curry powder
  • 2 tsp dried thyme
  • 4 bay leaf
  • 3 tsp salt
  • season cubes to taste

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Chop the tomatoes, onions, and bell pepper into chunks. Place into a blender/food processor together with the ginger, garlic, and scotch bonnet pepper. Then blitz into a smooth paste.
  2. Place a pot over medium heat, pour in the oil, tomato paste and add the blended mixture. Stir and allow to sizzle, then place a lid over it, reduce heat to low, and simmer for 20 minutes.
  3. After simmering for 20 minutes, remove the lid, then add the spices and seasonings (thyme,curry, bay leaf,seasoning cubes and salt). Stir altogether.
  4. Also pour in the rice and vegetable or meat stock and stir together.
  5. Place a piece of aluminium foil into the pot, covering the entire surface of the rice (this will keep the steam in), then place a lid over the pot. Cook for 20 minutes on low heat (Stirring occasionally to cook the rice evenly and also to prevent it from burning at the bottom).
  6. Remove lid, bin the aluminium foil and give the rice a gentle stir, then return the lid and cook for another 5 minutes.
  7. Now the rice should be ready to serve. Serve hot with your favourite sides and enjoy!
